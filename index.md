_👋 **Thank you for visiting my website. I appreciate you being here.**_

My work centers the invisible people and processes behind our digital lives. Sometimes that means building and supporting projects that nurture the care, maintenance, and attention needed for a better internet. Other times, it means tracing the infrastructures, industries, and supply chains that make our technologies possible.

I approach most things as an ethnographer first and a community-builder second. In recent years, I’ve turned to media art to reimagine new forms of public engagement, though I remain a writer at heart.

In 2025, I founded [STUDIO SANSHIN](https://www.aleesteele.com/services) as the home for my work in public interest technology, culture and research. You're also welcome to join an [internet infrastructure walk](https://luma.com/internet-intra-walks) or read my writing on [the poetic web](https://www.aleesteele.com/writing).

Over a little less than a decade, I’ve worked across open infrastructures, responsible technology, and cultural engagement – through roles, fellowships, collaborations, and more at the <a href="https://www.turing.ac.uk/">Alan Turing Institute</a>, <a href="https://www.redcross.org.uk">British Red Cross</a>, <a href="https://www.csis.org/dracopoulos-ideas-lab">Dracopoulos iDeas Lab</a>, <a href="https://www.internetsociety.org/fellowships/early-career/fellows/2022/">Internet Society</a>, <a href="https://www.missingmaps.org">Missing Maps</a>, <a href="https://okfn.org/">Open Knowledge Foundation</a>, <a href="https://openlifesci.org/">Open Seeds</a>, <a href="https://schoolofcommons.org">School of Commons</a>, <a href="https://www.software.ac.uk">Software Sustainability Institute</a>, <a href="http://transparencylab.org/">Transparency Lab</a>, <a href="https://transmediale.de/en">Transmediale</a>, <a href="https://book.the-turing-way.org">The Turing Way</a>, and <a href="https://www.wikimedia.de/unlock/">Wikimedia Deutschland’s Unlock Accelerator</a>. My previous research on digital humanitarism on OpenStreetMap can be found <a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/11zhVVBhJKjfgO79783oCDOdqs4OAflqg/view?usp=sharing">here</a>. I'm also an organiser of the <a href="https://research-fosdem.github.io">FOSDEM Open Research Devroom</a>, the largest open source conference in Europe.

I hold an MA from the <a href="http://graduateinstitute.ch/">Geneva Graduate Institute</a> and a BA from <a href="https://www.columbia.edu">Columbia University</a>. When I’m not online, I’m usually outside.
